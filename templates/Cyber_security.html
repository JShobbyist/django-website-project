Cyber threats continue to evolve at a rapid pace and pose increasingly significant risks to organizations.

As global cyber risk levels are spiraling out of control, the effectiveness of traditional cyber risk management is being called into question. Traditional cyber risk management often focuses on managing and addressing the cyber attacks independent from business and without consideration of their impact. Left unchecked, the growth in cyberattacks will become exponential. Organizations can no longer look to address these growing cyber-related dangers alone. BankingBook and Global Risk Institute jointly developed the Distribution Analysis for Information Risk (DAIR) framework for cyber risk quantification, giving consideration to asset vulnerabilities as well as business and systemic considerations. By implementing DAIR framework, organizations are able to:

  • Enhance a firm’s understanding of cyber risk exposure by highlighting where the highest dollar level of threat may be coming from;
  • Help management and boards set and monitor their cyber risk appetite, and make decisions based on the organization’s capacity, appetite, and actual risk level;
  • Better inform decisions relating to expenditures on cyber risk mitigation, economic capital allocation and insurance; and
  • Help management demonstrate to regulators that they are managing cyber risk in a comprehensive way.

CYBER RISK ASSESSMENT

The digitization of personal and business interactions is dramatically changing the cyber attack surface. There are three broad worksteps organized under Cyber Risk Assessment:

  • Enterprise-wide identification of critical assets: This workstep is used to segment critical cyber assets based on their contribution to achieving mission assurance. We use BankingBook's proprietary approach for identification and segmentation of networking and non-networking assets.
  • Critical assets' segmentation and ranking: In this workstep, we determine time-dependent analysis using multivariate causal approach to develop vulnerability ranking of the critical assets.
  • Loss data capture: The key objective of this workstep is to identify and collate internal historic data for threats, vulnerabilities, and incidents. Using event logs, questionnaires, workshops and working closely with experts, we segregates causal data in various dimensions, such as, threat sources, threats, vulnerability, and incidents. Incidents are then further analyzed to determine their knock-on effect. We then consolidate loss data to estimate severity, map loss data with assets and hierarchical risk taxonomy.

CYBER RISK QUANTIFICATION USING DAIR APPROACH

DAIR uses a three-dimensional approach to capture and size organizational cyber risk:

  • Operational cyber risk quantification for malicious and non-malicious cyber threats
  • Business cyber risk quantification to capture the knock-on effect of cyber incidents, e.g., loss of credibility and customer-base
  • Systemic cyber risk to measure the impact of cross-border software subversion, e.g., targeting of critically important systemic assets

Delivered in an easy-to-use, web-based front-end, Operational and Business cyber risk modules are included in BBA's ECAPLeader and Systemic cyber risk module is in our ScenarioFrontier application. Users have the option to either use cyber risk specific module or the entire suite.
